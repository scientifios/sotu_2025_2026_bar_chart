Q2: What changed between the 2025 and 2026 State of the Union addresses?

Topic keywords are extracted with a Bag-of-Words / n-gram + TF-IDF-inspired workflow, then grouped into semantic buckets using a Vector Space Model style clustering idea.

Storytelling focus

This mirrored bar chart compares the two addresses around the same topic axis. The upper bars represent 2026, while the lower bars represent 2025. The chart makes it easy to see which topics increased, decreased, or stayed central. In this result, 2026 shifts more toward anniversary/national identity, affordability, health care, and institutional claims, while 2025 emphasizes first-year executive action, border control, tariffs, energy, and government efficiency.

Colors encode semantic buckets. Subtopics under the same bucket use related shades, so the reader can compare detailed topics while still seeing larger themes. Hover over any topic label or bar to highlight both years and show the topic name at the top center of the visualization.

Hover a topic

Move the mouse over a topic label or bar to compare 2025 and 2026.

Method used

  1. Pre-processing: lowercasing, stop-word removal conceptually, phrase matching, and topic n-grams.
  2. Feature weighting: topic frequency is counted from extracted keyword sets; the design follows the course idea that TF-IDF helps identify discriminative terms.
  3. Semantic clustering: related topic features are placed into broader buckets, similar to using a Vector Space Model and clustering by semantic similarity.
  4. Visualization: a mirrored categorical bar chart shows 2026 above and 2025 below the same topic axis.
